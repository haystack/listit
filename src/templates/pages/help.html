Help

List.it is a free and open-source note-taking tool from MIT CSAIL that lets you safely, quickly, and easily write stuff down.

Creating A New Note

Just type into the top box to start creating a new note. When you are done, save the note to add it to your notelist.

List.it has several features to help you find old notes later. You can include tags to help you search for the note. Just type a hashtag "#" in front of a word and it becomes a tag. You can also pin a note, which keeps it at the top of your note list. You can use the button to save it with a pin, or you can type an exclamation point "!" at the beginning of your note and it will be automatically pinned.

Here are the buttons specific to the note creator:

ButtonDescription
Save note with a pin. This will keep your note pinned to the top of your notelist.
Save the note.

Here are the hotkeys used in the note creator:

HotkeyDescription
EscapeClear the contents of the note creator.
Control+EnterSave the note with the contents of the searchbar. If you have the separate searchbar open, this will add your search terms to the beginning of your note and save it.

Searching Your Notes

As you create a note, your notelist is automatically searched. You can search by tags and words and for a phrase by wrapping it in quotation marks. If you want to exclude a certain word or tag, you can type a minus sign "-" in front of it to filter it out.

Separate Searchbar

You also have the option of opening a separate searchbar. While it is open, your notelist will be filtered by the contents of the searchbar. Typing in the note creator will no longer filter your notes. If you start writing a new note and then open the searchbar, the contents of your note will automatically be added to the searchbar. This way you can keep your notes filtered while you write the rest of your new note.

You can open and close the searchbar by pressing the button in the controls, or you can use the following hotkeys anywhere in List.it:

HotkeyDescription
Control+FOpen the searchbar.
Control+XClose the separate searchbar. Now the note creator will go back to searching your notelist.

Here are the hotkeys used in the searchbar:

HotkeyDescription
EscClose the searchbar.
Control+FSelect contents of searchbar.
Control+XClose the searchbar.

Your Notes

Below the note creator you can see a list of all of the notes you have created. They are organized by when they were created, with your newest notes appearing higher in the list. Pinned notes stay at the very top. You can change whether or not a note is pinned by clicking on the star. You can reorganize your notes by clicking and dragging them to a new location, but you can't drag pinned notes.

You'll notice that tags in your notes look like links. If you click them, they'll be added to the searchbar. If they were already in the searchbar, then they will be removed. This will make it easier to filter your notes.

Here are the buttons you will use while viewing your notelist:

Delete this note.

Pin this note.

The Studies

The team here at MIT CSAIL created List.it as a simple note-taking tool, but we're also interested in studying the way people take notes. These studies help us to make a better program for you! When you sign up for a List.it account you can choose to opt in to our study. Our researchers will be able to study your notes. No one outside of our research group will be able to access any of your information unless you give us explicit permission.

Get more information about the studies here.

Settings

On the options page you can customize List.it by setting your preferences. There is an option to 'Collapse Notes.' By default, the List.it sidebar shows the full text of each of your notes. If you check this option then you will only see the first line of each note. This will allow you to see more of your notes at once. You will still be able to click on a note to expand and edit it.

There is another option to 'Hide text format buttons.' If you check this, the editor buttons that exist while creating and editing a note will no longer appear. This will make List.it look much simpler. The hotkeys for formatting text will still work without the buttons. You may need to reopen the List.it sidebar before this change will take effect.
